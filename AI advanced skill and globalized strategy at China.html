1. Features and Challenges of the AI Development Environment

China's access to **major global AI services** like Google (Gemini) and OpenAI (ChatGPT) is **blocked by the 'Great Firewall'**.

Challenge: Technical and Information Constraints

The closed environment restricts developers from accessing the latest global technology and information.

Counter-Intuition: The Engine for a 'Self-Reliant Technology Ecosystem'

Conversely, this is a strong driving force for China to build a **self-reliant technology ecosystem**, reducing external dependence.

2. Core Strategy A: Securing Massive, Exclusive Domestic Data

Strengthening Data Sovereignty

All data generated by hundreds of millions of Chinese users (search, SNS, payments) is accumulated exclusively within the platforms of domestic companies like Baidu, Alibaba, and Tencent.

Gaining Competitive Edge

By securing a **vast pool of high-quality data**, essential for AI model training, without external competition, they rapidly enhance the performance of their AI models.

2. Core Strategy B: Active 'Self-Reliant Innovation' and Platform Nurturing

Accelerating Technological Independence

Whenever foreign technology (like the OpenAI API) is blocked, Chinese companies quickly attract developers to their own models (Ernie, Qwen), thereby **reducing foreign dependence.**

Localization of the Development Environment

To bypass slow or blocked overseas servers when installing Python libraries, they use **Mirror Servers** operated by domestic companies and universities to maintain stable development efficiency.

2. Core Strategy C: National Infrastructure and Policy Support

  • Designation as Strategic Industry: AI is designated as a key national industry, receiving strong financial and administrative support.
  • AI Computing Infrastructure Investment: Massive investment in essential physical foundations for AI R&D, including data centers, cloud platforms, and supercomputing facilities.
  • Defending Technological Sovereignty: All this investment is directly linked to the national goal of achieving 'technological self-reliance' against U.S. technology sanctions.
