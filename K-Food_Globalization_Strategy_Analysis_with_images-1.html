K-Food Globalization: Common and Differentiating Factors

Based on the Netflix series 'Bon appétit, your majesty' (Chef of the Tyrant)

The Role of 'Chef of the Tyrant' in K-Food Diffusion

① Cooking in Extreme Environments

Demonstrates K-Food's **'adaptability'** and **'vitality'** by preparing dishes using local ingredients in unfamiliar settings.

② Conveying 'Jeong' (Affection) and Culture

Transmits the **'emotional value'** of K-Culture by showing comfort and communication through food, beyond simple taste.

③ Building Global Consensus

Boosts accessibility and creates demand by revealing **practical recipes** that anyone can follow.

K-Food Commonalities: The Foundation of All Success

1. Sincerity (Authenticity)

Authentic portrayal of Korean food culture that emphasizes time and effort (e.g., Kimchi aging, making fermented pastes).

2. Hallyu Content Linkage (Synergy)

Powerful storytelling effect achieved by being consumed alongside K-Culture content (Dramas, Movies, Entertainment).

All K-Foods stand on two pillars: **'Cultural Context'** and **'Sincerity'**.

K-Food Differentiating Factors (Global Positioning)

① Fermentation-based Health (Health Focus)

Highlights the **probiotic** value of fermented products like Kimchi and *Jang* (pastes). (Aligns with Western wellness trends).

② Flavor Versatility (Flavor Flexibility)

Spiciness of *Gochujang* (chili paste) and umami of *Ganjang* (soy sauce) combine with foreign ingredients to create **new fusion menus**.

③ HMR Innovation (HMR Leadership)

High technological capability in K-Food HMR (Ramen, instant rice, meal kits) maximizes **accessibility**.

Differentiated Narrative: 'Chef of the Tyrant's' Image Innovation

Old Image: Static 'Fine Dining'

Centered on traditional Royal Court cuisine or expensive restaurants, resulting in **low accessibility** and high barriers to entry.

New Image: Dynamic 'Street Kitchen'

  • **Mobility:** Proves K-Food can be made anywhere globally with just a pot and a knife.
  • **Localization:** Presents **'Lived-in K-Food'** using local ingredients, distinct from mere fusion.
  • **Approachability:** Shifts perception from complex expert cooking to a dish **'made with a friend'**.

Vision: 'Study Pony Campus' for K-Food

Inspired by the drama 'Unicorn ni Notte', this proposal envisions a **Free Virtual K-Food School** to empower global users to adapt and evolve Korean culinary traditions using local ingredients.

Business Model: B2B Localization Data Solution

The platform's primary value is not advertising, but generating unique **localization data**. This data (e.g., user search patterns, foreign ingredient substitutions, flavor trend evolution) can be sold as a strategic resource to Korean food manufacturers for product development (HMRs, sauces) and market entry planning.

B2B Data Value: Monetizing Localization Insights

The core revenue model of 'Study Pony Campus' focuses on monetizing the unique behavioral data generated by global users adapting K-Food recipes.

Data Points Collected (AI Trainer Focus)

  • **Ingredient Substitution Rate:** Which local ingredients globally replace common Korean ones (e.g., substituting Napa cabbage for local greens).
  • **Flavor Profile Evolution:** Regional spice reduction/addition trends (e.g., sweetness vs. salt preference).
  • **Content Engagement:** Which K-Food cultural stories or drama clips drive the highest conversion to cooking attempts.

Target Clients & Value Proposition (CSM Focus)

  • **HMR Manufacturers:** Gain intelligence on flavor adjustment needs for mass market HMRs in specific regions.
  • **Sauce/Paste Exporters:** Receive real-time data on the optimal spice level and packaging size for local distribution.
  • **Cultural Agencies:** Access quantified evidence of K-Culture content impact on food consumption.
