Methodology to Identify Inefficient Inquiries (e.g., Black Consumers) and Minimize Response Load

Chart Interpretation:

  • Sharp Drop in Staff Intervention Needs: As inquiries pass through AI Stage 1 (RAG) and Stage 2 (LSTM) filtering, most simple/repeated inquiries are handled automatically or by the system, reducing the final volume reaching experienced staff to less than 5%.
  • Shift in Staff Role: Instead of repetitive tasks, staff focus on 'High-Risk Black Consumers' or 'Policy Improvement Cases' identified by the AI, maximizing productivity.

Detail of the AI-Based 3-Step CS Efficiency Strategy

Stage 1: RAG-Based 'Information Filtering and Standard Response'

Goal: AI accurately handles simple or standardized inquiries in the first phase, minimizing staff intervention.

Technology: RAG (Retrieval-Augmented Generation) - Real-time lookup of the latest refund policies and terms to generate the most accurate response to customer inquiries.

Effect: AI immediately presents accurate regulations (reasons for non-cancellation), resolving customer misunderstanding and blocking repeated requests based on the expectation of a "staff error."

Technology: Core Keyword Filtering - Extracts keywords like 'cancellation', 'refund', 'not possible', etc., to prioritize the RAG system's response.

Stage 2: LSTM/ML-Based 'Malicious Pattern and Intent Analysis'

Goal: AI analyzes the customer's past history and inquiry patterns to predict and classify whether they are a 'black consumer' making repeated, unreasonable requests. (Core to efficient handling of difficult customers)

Technology: LSTM (Time-Series Prediction) - Learns time-series data such as past inquiry counts and repeated requests despite previous resolution.

Effect: Assigns an 'Abusive Pattern Prediction' score (Anomaly Score) to decide whether to escalate high-risk cases ("80%+ chance of repeating the inquiry after resolution") to Stage 3.

Technology: Qualitative Text Analysis (Sentiment Analysis) - Detects strong dissatisfaction expressions like threats or abuse to classify risk urgently and halt automatic response.

Technology: Policy Violation History Mapping - Links past history of fraud attempts or terms violations to clearly identify high-risk customers, instructing the AI to use firm, principled response templates.

Stage 3: AI Data-Based 'Rapid Final Decision and Response by Expert Staff'

Goal: Escalated 'black consumer' cases from Stage 2 are transferred to experienced staff with AI analysis data to resolve the dispute.

Stakeholder: Experienced CS Staff - Utilizes AI reports summarizing Stage 2 LSTM analysis results, RAG policy evidence, and all past interaction records.

Effect: Enables rapid and consistent response; staff can grasp complex history within 5 minutes, initiating a firm refusal or legal action preparation, thereby reducing dispute time.

Stakeholder: Dedicated Macro/Template - Uses a warning template with legal effect ("This is the company's final decision, further repeated inquiries will not be responded to") for customers with an 'LSTM Score X or higher', ending the dispute and reducing staff emotional drain.
