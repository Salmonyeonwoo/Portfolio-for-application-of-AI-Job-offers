The AI K-Culture Scripter Platform

A Strategic Blueprint for Converting Passive K-Content Viewing into Active Global Economic Output

Executive Summary: The Core Inefficiency

  • The global success of Hallyu (K-Content) is primarily driven by passive viewing on OTT platforms.
  • A crucial inefficiency exists: this massive cultural consumption fails to translate into direct, measurable economic activity (tourism, F&B, goods).
  • This policy addresses the systemic weak link between cultural curiosity and economic conversion.

The Proposed Solution

AI K-Culture Scripter
An Interactive Culture-Economy Platform

We propose an AI-based platform integrated into OTT streams (e.g., Netflix) using:

  • Vision AI: To recognize objects, food, and locations in real-time.
  • Generative AI: To provide context, recipes, and translations.

The goal is to create seamless, real-time, "click-to-purchase" pathways to immediately capture and monetize viewer curiosity.

Policy & Contest Context

2025 Future Korea Idea Contest

This proposal was submitted for the "AI Great Transformation Policy Idea for Technology-Led Growth" theme, soliciting creative and effective policies for Korea's future.

Category: AI Growth Economic Vitality

Our policy aligns with this mandate by positioning the cultural sector not just as soft power, but as a sophisticated, data-driven engine for new revenue.

The Consumption Barrier: Passive Viewing

Current OTTs offer only superficial metadata (actors, music). Viewers with deep cultural curiosity must interrupt their viewing to conduct separate external searches.

This disruption creates a significant friction point, leading to a high rate of lost consumption opportunities.

This policy aims to eradicate this friction and dramatically increase the economic yield per viewer.

The Deeper Problem: The Nuance Gap

The Linguistic Gap

Simple translated subtitles are insufficient. They fail to convey rich cultural context, such as Korean honorifics (존칭) and humility (겸양). This "linguistic flattening" hinders deep cultural understanding.

AI as a Cultural Diplomat

The platform will use AI to deliver real-time, context-sensitive explanations of these nuances, moving Hallyu from simple entertainment to educational engagement and enhancing long-term soft power.

A Technologically Mature Solution

Vision AI

For real-time object, food, and location recognition directly within the video stream. This technology is commercially ready.

Generative AI

For context-specific information generation, recipe creation, and high-quality, nuanced multilingual translation (e.g., Google Gemini Pro).

Commercially Viable

The necessary architecture is mature. This policy is achievable now, without requiring long-term basic R&D.

The User Flow: Converting Curiosity to Commerce

The platform's value is a low-friction process that bridges interest with transaction.

  1. Viewing: A user watches a K-Drama.
  2. AI Recognition: The system identifies an item (e.g., *Dalkbokkeum-tang*).
  3. User Click: The user clicks the recognized item.
  4. Info Window: An interactive popup offers options: "VIEW RECIPE," "BUY MEAL-KIT," "FIND RESTAURANT."
  5. E-Commerce: Clicking "BUY MEAL-KIT" links to a store, creating economic value.

Consumption Pillar 1: K-Food Linkage

For Viewers Abroad

The AI provides instant links to purchase ingredients or pre-packaged meal-kits from online shopping malls, directly driving K-Food exports.

For Viewers in Korea

The AI uses location data to recommend nearby Korean restaurants (Hansik-dang) that serve the featured dish, driving immediate local economic activity.

Consumption Pillar 2: K-Tourism Linkage

The platform converts cultural curiosity into high-value tourism demand by providing detailed information on drama filming locations.

It creates an experiential loop by:

  • Integrating with Korea Tourism Organization (KTO) official products.
  • Providing customized guides to visitors.
  • Offering opportunities for free food sampling (like in *Dae Jang Geum*) at major cultural sites.

Consumption Pillar 3: K-Product Linkage

The AI immediately recognizes PPL (Product Placement) items, such as K-Beauty and K-Fashion, used by characters.

It provides a direct, traceable link to the official seller or e-commerce channel.

This seamless connection eliminates external searches, maximizing the conversion rate for consumer goods exports and capturing impulse buys.

Governance & Risk Management

The Core Risk

The primary risk is legal and commercial complexity surrounding Intellectual Property (IP) rights, content copyright, and PPL utilization rights.

The Mitigation

A Mandatory Revenue Sharing (수익 공유) framework must be legally defined and agreed upon *before* the platform's construction.

The Goal: 상생

This creates an incentivized, "all-participants-win" ecosystem (상생하는 생태계) to ensure sustainability and stakeholder buy-in.

Quantifiable Economic Impact

Reduces Marketing Costs

Enables direct, instantaneous product exposure to a high-intent global audience, dramatically reducing the need for costly traditional overseas marketing campaigns.

Creates Data-Driven Strategy

Captures invaluable, real-time data on global preferences (K-Food, K-Beauty, tourism) to establish sophisticated, proactive, and data-based export strategies.

Strategic Recommendation

This policy leverages cultural dominance to solve a critical market failure, positioning K-Content as a primary engine for the nation's 'AI Growth Economic Vitality'.
We strongly recommend immediate government backing to formalize the Collaborative Governance Model and the Mandatory Revenue Sharing Agreement.
